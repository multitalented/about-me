Who Am I?

Composer

I am very much a self-taught composer with almost a lifetime of experience doing it. I’ve always naturally been the intuitive creative type, and as long as I can remember, I’ve been writing music. Ever since I laid my little fingers on a keyboard, I was fascinated. My parents tell me that I was making my little toy keyboard sound good when I was just a toddler. Eventually I got a light-up keyboard and starting teaching myself songs, but I begged for lessons. Finally when I was 11, I got a great teacher who mentored me for a few years, teaching me how to harness my songwriting and improvisational skills. I was donated an upright piano by her friend, I would perform at nursing homes and talent shows, and I had 3 “albums” recorded at her friend’s home studio. I taught myself how to notate and arrange music in a pirated copy of Finale. Unfortunately, when I was 15, my family moved 1000 miles away, and I lost that encouragement, since my parents were not as supportive of this dream. Fortunately, I never stopped playing video games or writing music, I just stopped putting myself out there, keeping my music private. Ever since doing my first game jam in January 2022 though, I’ve decided to put myself back out there!

Engineer

I went to school for engineering partly because I was doing what my parents thought I should do, but also I convinced myself I didn’t need to go to school for music since I had already spent my childhood essentially studying it. Since I continued to play piano, I never lost sight of staying true to myself. After my first couple years as an engineer in the workforce, I realized I needed to figure out what I wanted from my career because I did not find meaning in my job. Eventually I obtained a masters in engineering where I studied acoustics, signals, optimization, and C++, which laid a good foundation for audio. I found love at the intersection of engineering and music! Now I use my technical background to learn audio engineering as needed. I also work as an engineer for the government, where I get a lot more meaning from what I do, since I realized how important it was for me to have a purpose in what I do.

Writer

I always loved writing in just about any capacity. Although I’ve done a LOT of technical writing as an engineer, when I was growing up I wrote a lot of poems, short stories, journal entries, and even some comics. I’ve always enjoyed finding creative ways to spin things with word play, even into grad school, when I spent hours pouring over code and results for a project. I would get to the final report, and finally feel like I could get away from the constraints of fact to the open world of writing. Slipping humor into my reports always helped me get me through the gruel of technical writing and was often appreciated by graders. These days, I like to write in various forms, but my favorite is coming up with comics and parody.

Gamer

Very closely tied to my love for music, growing up on video games is where it all began. I watched my brother play Mega Man and Mario games, mesmerized by the music most of all. My love for video games has only increased as I got older and moved from classic consoles to PC and modern gaming. I absolutely credit those early days as having a strong influence on my songwriting style. Besides MegaMan, my favorite games/series include Zelda, Professor Layton, BioShock, Portal, Super Meat Boy, Fable, Dishonored, Super Mario, TIS-100, and Amnesia.

Language Learner

My passion for languages started in my mid 20s, when I realized I had not explored myself more in college and delved into French and classic literature. Eventually when I did my masters at Georgia Tech, I studied at their campus in Metz, France, where I put my French into practice. Since then, I’ve used the methods discussed in Fluent Forever to learn Spanish and Russian. I love finding the patterns in a language and learning how to think differently. It’s so neat!

Self-taught

I love learning, and I love helping people, so when I learn new things, I love sharing about it! My favorite YouTube content is educational and I’ve done a number of courses between that, Udemy and Pluralsight on various mostly game and tech topics, such as Unity, game dev, web dev, SQL, networking (IT), cybersecurity, and Wwise. Grad school really furthered my belief that I can teach myself anything, so I teach myself what I need to know when I need to know it.

Art Enthusiast

When I was at the University of Florida, I worked as a figure model for the College of Fine Arts. I loved being close to art by working with students in their figure painting, drawing, and ceramics courses. Some projects required bringing in props and costume pieces to help set up a scene for the students, which I always enjoyed. Now I just appreciate artists so much for what they do. The most art I do is comic stick figures, bad acrylic paintings, and geometrical/mathematical art.

Reader

I am always reading something, and I believe in the power of gaining knowledge, inspiration, and empathy through reading others’ stories and perspectives. I’m happy to discuss and share my book recommendations any time!

Cat Lover

Most importantly I am a lover of animals, and specifically cats. If you come across my gamer tag FergiesHuman, or more properly, “Fergie’s Human”, it means my cat’s name is Fergie and I am her human. We’ve been in a committed relationship now for 13 years and she is like my little Pokemon. If you don’t love cats, meet mine and you will fall in love. Besides Fergie, I also love pretty much every cat I ever meet, but I am very fond of tortoiseshell and calico coats.
