Who Am I?

Composer

Long before I even knew what engineering meant, I thought I was going to be a composer! Ever since I laid my eyes and my little fingers on a keyboard (a toy one to begin with), I was fascinated. My parents told me that I was making my little yellow toy keyboard sound good making up my own songs when I was about 4 years old. Eventually I got a light-up Casio keyboard and starting teaching myself songs with the song bank, but I begged for lessons. Coming from poor and uneducated families, they weren’t the most excited about buying a piano and paying for lessons. Luckily I found a great teacher who doted on me for a few years, teaching me how to harness my songwriting and improvise. When I was 15 and moved 1000 miles away with my family, I lost my teacher, and this likely had a huge influence on my losing this direction, as my parents were not as supportive. Fortunately, I never stopped playing or writing, I just stopped putting myself out there for a long time, keeping my music private.

Engineer

I ended up going to school for engineering because like many out there, I chose to do what others thought I should do. This contributed to some major depression for a good while, but there was still many positives that came out of this path. After my first couple years as an engineer in the workforce, I realized I needed to figure out what I wanted from working because I was not fulfilled. Eventually I obtained a masters in engineering from Georgia Institute of Technology, where I furthered my knowledge in acoustics, signals, optimization, and advanced programming. These skills helped me appreciate music even more and made me see a love I had at the intersection of engineering and music. If I had known about the field of audio engineering, I probably would’ve studied that, but instead I teach myself what I need to know as I develop my recording studio!

Creative Writer

I always loved writing in just about any capacity. Growing up I wrote a lot of poems, short stories, and essays. I would always try to slip in some cheek, even into grad school, when I spent hours pouring over MATLAB code for a project. Slipping humor into my reports always helped me get me through the gruel of technical writing and was usually appreciated by the graders. These days, I tend to enjoy writing parody/satire, usually as a gift for someone or simply inspired by my day to day life at work.

Gamer

Very closely tied to my love for music, writing stories, and coding, growing up on video games is where it all began. I watched my brother play Mega Man and Mario games, mesmerized by the music and action. My love for video games only increased as I got older and moved from classic consoles to PC and modern gaming. I absolutely credit those early days as having a strong influence on my songwriting style.

Language Learner

I know, is this really relevant? “Deanna, everyone does Duolingo”, you may say, rolling your eyes. No, I don’t actually use Duolingo. I use a host of other tools, books, and methods to immerse myself in a language I want to learn. My passion for languages started when I was at a major low point in my life at the age of 25 and realized I was making major life decisions based on what someone else thought I should do. I felt like I had missed some real opportunities and not explored myself more. At that point, I delved deep into a number of things, including French, reading classic literature, tennis, web development. Eventually when I did my masters at Georgia Tech, I decided to do some of it at their campus in Metz, France, where I got to put my French into practice! Since then, I’ve been focused on Spanish and Russian. One of my favorite parts of learning a language is learning the culture and nuance. I love seeing the patterns in a language and learning how to think differently. It’s so neat!
